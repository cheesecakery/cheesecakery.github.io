The Journey

I presume that you have dared to venture on this page with the knowledge that is required. There are several principles I must bestow upon you if we are to continue to embark on this journey.

Firstly, the importance of wider reading. If you feel uncomfortable on the subject of the Lancastrians, Yorkists or Tudors, I would highly suggest taking a moment or two, a pen and quill or two, and engaging with the subject matter listed below. Remember, this is for your benefit.

  • The War of the Roses
  • Richard III
  • Henry VII

Secondly, and perhaps most importantly, you must have the desire within you to succeed followed by a passion for the Sir Cheesecakians. This quiz is no walk in the park, but with dedicated time and effort, I hope that you will come to cherish its brilliance.

I wish you good luck.

It was this very marriage which allowed my great great-grandson, James VI, to take the throne in 1603 and finally unite the two crowns.

Q2: On hearing the second question, you answered that the divine marriage alliance I was able to secure was between my son, Henry, and the Spanish princess, the Katherine of Aragon. You were incorrect. I regret to say that you were very close to a correct answer, but initially, it was my eldest son Arthur who would be betrothed to Katherine. It was only upon Arthur's far too early death that Henry, and I mean soon-to-be Henry VIII, would encroach on his wife. Funnily enough, Henry had to seek papal dispensation from the pope as it was seen rather immoral to marry your brother's wife, dead or not.

Q2: On hearing the second question, you answered that the divine marriage alliance I was able to secure was between myself and my splendid wife, Elizabeth of Anjou. I regret to inform that you were incorrect, and that I was actually married to the Elizabeth of York. I was able to use this marriage for my own propaganda, claiming to be uniting the two previously aggravated families, the Yorkists, and the Lancastrians, I myself having a weak link to the Lancastrian dynasty. That is where the Tudor rose comes into play, symbolising this union.
